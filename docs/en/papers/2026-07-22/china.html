China Watch: Policy Updates and Social & Legal Focus

This edition of China Watch covers the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security's recruitment of 13,000 postdoctoral researchers in the first half of the year, the State Council Taiwan Affairs Office's responses to DPP's slander of Hong Kong's rule of law and restrictions on mainland spouses' political participation, and the investigation of a former vice chairman of Zhuhai Political Consultative Conference. In society and law, a fire at a vehicle market in Zhengzhou was found to be arson, Ningxia built a legal culture park, Tibet's Nagqu promoted cultural tourism, and many regions experienced heavy rain. Society & Law: Fire, Legal Culture Park, Tourism & Weather

A fire at Zhengzhou Hongda International Vehicle Market was confirmed as arson, resulting in 2 deaths and 2 injuries. Ningxia's Yellow River Legal Culture Belt Theme Park integrates law with agriculture and tourism. Nagqu, Tibet held a cultural tourism promotion conference. Meanwhile, heavy rain is forecast for Guangxi and the middle-lower Yangtze region, requiring disaster prevention.

E-Law Together, Law Enriches Frontier | A New Legal Landscape Emerging from Rice Fields in Helan, Ningxia 中国新闻网社会

The Yellow River Legal Culture Belt Theme Park in Ningxia integrates legal publicity with paddy field landscapes, using rice plants to form legal slogans, allowing visitors to experience legal culture while sightseeing. The park embeds legal education into agricultural experiences and rural tourism, innovating legal publicity methods.
