Lead Story
Harvard scientists turn a silicon chip into a DNA writing machine

Harvard scientists have created a silicon chip that can write dozens of DNA sequences simultaneously using electricity and water-based enzymes, offering a cleaner alternative to conventional DNA manufacturing. The breakthrough could eventually support portable DNA-writing devices and massive DNA data storage, though new chemistry is needed to scale.
