Lead Story
Staple-shaped particles can become strong or fall apart in seconds, pointing to recyclable buildings

Scientists have found that staple-shaped particles can tangle together to create a material that is both strong and flexible. Unlike conventional materials, these particles can be locked into a sturdy structure or rapidly unraveled using vibrations, with potential applications in recyclable buildings and reconfigurable structures.
