Black hole winds may be robbing giant galaxies of their future stars

Using XRISM observations of galaxy NGC 4151, astronomers found strong evidence that supermassive black holes unleash powerful winds that blow away the raw material needed for star formation, potentially explaining why some of the universe's largest galaxies have far fewer stars than expected.
